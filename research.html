Research Interests

Past and present topics

Clusters of Galaxies

I am interested in using clusters of galaxies to explore the nature of dark matter. By combining weak lensing maps with X-ray observations, we can compare the total mass of galaxy clusters to the baryonic mass traced by X-ray emission. This helps understand the baryonic mass fraction distribution in clusters.

Telescope and Optical System Simulations

How good is my telescope? This is the question behind telescope and optical system simulations. I help with the improvement and implementation of telescopes to PhoSim . These simulations let us explore how real-world effects—like dust, the atmosphere, and even the thickness of silicon sensors—can influence the performance of the telescopes we design and rely on.

Red Stragglers

Red stragglers are an extraordinarily rare and mysterious type of star that has only recently been classified as a distinct stellar type (Gellar et al. 2017). Their origins cannot be explained by single-star evolution and remain unexplained today. Previously, I studied and identified these stars in globular clusters.
