About Me

Professional History

In addition to my academic work, I am a commissioned officer in the United States Army, where I specialize in communication and information systems planning. I was a part of Operation Spartan Shield (2023) and fought wildfires in Northern California (2020). I have received awards such as the Army Commendation Medal and the California Commendation Medal.

I am committed to science and math education and outreach. At San Francisco State University (SFSU), I helped operate the planetarium and the school’s observatory. In San Francisco, I was a science and math teacher for Fusion Academy of San Francisco, and I tutored math in Chinatown. I am a strong advocate for science, service, and education.

Personal History

I was born in San Francisco, CA, and I grew up in Yorba Linda, CA. From middle school to high school, I was heavily involved in band, where I was in the 2017 Rose Parade. I got interested in astronomy after I attended an observational astronomy course at Fullerton Community College. After seeing what a dark sky truly is, I was hooked on astronomy.

Outside of astronomy, I have a wide range of hobbies. Some include fixing up cars, riding motorcycles, learning languages, hiking, traveling, and obstacle course racing. My ongoing hobby goals are to run a marathon on every continent and to participate in the World’s Toughest Mudder and score above 50 miles.
